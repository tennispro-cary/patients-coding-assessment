SDPD officer and suspect rushed to hospital after being run over by car

Posted: Updated:
By Jarrett Wright, Digital Content Producer
Email
Connect
jwright@kfmb.com

SAN DIEGO (NEWS 8) - An SDPD officer and another person were taken to the hospital Thursday after being run over by a car.

The officer had a vandalism suspect restrained on the ground just before 5 a.m. when the two were hit by the vehicle in the 3500 block of Midway Drive.  

Prior to the accident, the officer had been chasing the suspect on foot. A witness told News 8 that when the officer caught up to the suspect he pinned him to the ground in the middle of the street where there was no lighting around them.

Both the officer and the suspect were immediately taken to the hospital. Authorities said the officer was in serious but stable condition while the suspect was in critical condition with life threatening injuries.

The driver remained on scene. San Diego Police say the woman who hit the officer and suspect was not speeding and was not under the influence of drugs or alcohol. 

Southbound lanes on Rosecrans Street were shut down for a time between Sports Arena Boulevard and Midway Drive following the incident.
